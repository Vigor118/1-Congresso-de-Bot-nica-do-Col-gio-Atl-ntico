Estudo sobre a influência do substrato no crescimento de Lactuca sativa

  • Casinha, J.
  • Domingos, P.
  • Henriques, J.
  • Nunes, T.
  • Santos, P.

Hydroponics is a technique used for growing plants in nutritious solutions with or without the use of a medium that provides support for the plant. The Lactuca sativa plant was chosen to be used on this project due to the fact that it was the pioneer in the hydroponics field, and due to having a short cycle of production and being easily accepted on the market. The nutrient film technique (NFT) is defined as method for agricultural production. When using this technique, a plant develops its root system partially under recycled water with all the elements needed dissolved in it. Vertical farming systems (VFS) have been proposed as an engineering solution to increase productivity per unit of cultivated land area by extending agricultural production to the vertical dimension.
The main objectives of this project are studying the difference between using a vertical or a nutrient film technique system and studying the effects of the different nutritious solutions on the plants that were placed in the vertical and in the nutrient film technique system.
To proceed with this investigation, twenty Lactuca sativa were used and devided into two groups, each one with ten. One group was placed on a vertical system and the other on an NFT one. After that, the height, number of leaves, luminosity and moisture were recorded weekly.
During the project the variables were controlled, in order to maintain stability throughout the project, with the intention of not affecting the growth of the Lactuca sativa in different ways, making their comparison possible. Despite the fact that they all had the same height and number of leaves in the beginning of the project, there are huge differences between the NFT system and the Vertical system. The values of Lactuca sativa in the NFT system were higher than the values of Lactuca sativa the Vertical system, proving that the cultivation of Lactuca sativa in the NFT system is more effective.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Nutrient Film Technique; Vertical Farming System; Lactuca sativa.






Técnicas de hidroponia por fibra de coco comparativamente com técnica de hidroponia por sistema vertical em Lactuca sativa

  • Bruno, J.
  • Duarte, F.
  • Farinha, M.
  • Silva, D.
  • Silva, L.

The Lactuca sativa is the most important crop in the group of leafy vegetables. It is characterized by considerable morphological and genetic variation. To study this species, the Hydroponics technique was used. Hydroponics is the cultivation of plants in nutrient-enriched water, with or without the mechanical support of an inert medium such as sand, gravel, or perlite. In combination with greenhouses, hydroponics is becoming increasingly popular, especially in the United States, Canada, Western Europe, and Japan.
To accomplish the goal, two different methods were utilized: coconut fibre substrate and lettuce growth vertically. A total of 20 lettuces were studied, half cultivated in a horizontal system with coconut fibre substrate and the other half vertically with no soil. Data was collected every week. The length and number of leaves was measured, along with pH level, temperature, humidity and conductivity.
After harvested, it is possible to conclude that the horizontal system is more efficient. In the vertical system, the lower lettuces didn’t grow as much as the others. This happened due to thenutritional solution not reaching every lettuce in the same amount.

Keywords: Lactuca sativa; hydroponics; nutritional solutions; coconut substract fibre.






Estudo do desenvolvimento da espécie Lactuca sativa (alface) em hidroponia (nutrition film technique) comparativamente ao solo

  • Barroso, C.
  • Nogueira, B.
  • Nogueira, D.
  • Rosado, A.
  • Silva, I.

Hydroponics is a method to grow plants without soil, just using water and mineral salts. The purpose of this study was to compare the growth of the lettuce (Lactuca sativa) in hydroponics by nutrient film technique (NFT) and in the soil.
The project started in January. The first step was to plant the lettuces. The lettuces were measured, the number of leaves was counted and the climatic and chemical conditions (ph level, electric conductivity, brightness, humidity and temperature of the air) were measured throughout the weeks until march. On the day of the harvest, the lettuces were weighed.
The lettuces from NFT were heavier and slightly bigger than the lettuces from the soil. The lettuces from NFT grew more on the third, fourth and last weeks when the ph level, electric conductivity and brightness were higher. The lettuces from the soil grew more on the fourth and last weeks for the same reasons.
It’s possible to conclude that the lettuces grew slightly more in the nutrient film technique.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Lactuca sativa; nutrient film technique; soil.






Estudo sobre a influência do substrato em Lactuca sativa

  • Catalão, L.
  • Cruz, T.
  • Luís, R.
  • Mendes, B.
  • Pereira, M.
  • Teixeira, R.

Hydroponics is the technique of growing plants without soil, in which the mineral elements essential for the growth and development of the plants are supplied through a nutrient solution. In hydroponics the roots can be suspended in liquid medium, nutrient film technique (NFT), or supported in inert substrate (coconut fiber).
This project investigates the difference in growth of a Lactuca sativa in two hydroponics techniques/methods: NFT and coconut fiber, to evaluate which is the most efficient.
Twenty Lactuca sativa were selected. Ten were planted utilizing NFT, while the others where grown in coconut fiber. All were observed and analyzed through their whole development process until fully-grown.
The results and analysis exhibited clear variations among the plants studied, indicating differences between the two hydroponics methods used. Lactuca sativa grown using NFT were always bigger, compared to the ones grown with coconut fiber. It was concluded, based on the research done, that NFT is a more efficient hydroponic method/technique than the coconut fiber.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Nutrient film technique (NFT); Coconut fiber; Lactuca sativa.






Crescimento da Lactuca Sativa em diferentes substratos: Fibra de coco e Solo

  • Alves, C.
  • Ganhão, M.
  • Loureiro, K.
  • Marques, A.
  • Silva, M.

Hydroponics is the technique of growing soilless plants, where the essential minerals are given by means of a nutrient solution. The roots can be suspended in liquid medium or supported on an inert substrate (rock wool, coconut fiber, among others). The objective of our project is to study the influence of the different substrates on the growth of Lactuca sativa.
The experiment was done by using coconut fiber and soil with three nutrient solutions. In each substrate ten Lactuca sativa were planted. Each week, the group went to the greenhouse one day to check on the Lactuca sativa, to regulate the nutritional solution and to measure the physical parameters. After observing the results, it was clear that under the same conditions but different soils, the plants have similar number of leaves, but there were differences in the number of plants that reached this number.
Summing up, we can conclude that the coconut fiber was more efficient than the soil because there was a constant growth of the plants in the coconut fiber and in the soil the growth was irregular, suffering changes over time.

Keywords: Hydroponics; nutritional solution; Lactuca sativa; coconut fiber; soil.

Estudo do crescimento de Fragaria vesca em técnicas de hidroponia – Fibra de Coco

  • Caeiro, F.
  • Martins, R.
  • Pascoal, M.
  • Romão, M.

Hydroponics is a soilless technique used to grow culture. This study compares the growth of Fragaria vesca, the most common strawberry in Europe, in two different systems: a hydroponic one using coconut fiber and one using regular soil and plant vases. All the plants were cultivated in a greenhouse using a pre-determined solution previously studied.
The results led to the conclusion that the strawberry’s growth was similar in both subtracts, even though the hydroponic system seemed to be more efficient. The plants revealed high level of growth in just a few weeks, making it possible to conclude that the soilless system is not only adequate for this culture, but also beneficial.
In the future soilless systems may become necessary to grow vegetables since they allow to have a higher production per area unit and this study adds to provethat it is possible to grow plants without polluting nor depending on the soil.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Fragaria vesca; coconut fiber; soilless systems.






Estudo do crescimento de Fragaria vesca em técnicas de hidroponia – “Nutrient Film Technique” (NFT)

  • Pires, B.
  • Ramalho, R.
  • Sousa, S.

Hydroponics is a technique of growing plants without soil, in which the nutrients and minerals are provided by nutrient solutions, in order to guarantee a healthy growth for the plants. This study consisted in cultivating ten plants of Fragaria vesca both in Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) and nutritious soil, and measuring their high, number of leaves, shoots, flowers and fruits during eight weeks. This experiment meant to compare the systems and find out which is more efficient for the growth of this species. After the eight weeks of study, it is possible to conclude that the plants on NFT had more shoots and flowers than the ones on nutritious soil, but these were bigger and had a higher number of leaves. The experiment helped reaching the conclusion that both systems used are effective, in which the plants on nutritious soil grew higher and the ones in NFT had more leaves.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Fragaria vesca; Nutrient Film Technique (NFT); nutrient solutions; technique of growing plants without soil.

Comparação do Desenvolvimento Eruca sativa em hidroponia por “Nutrient Film Technique”(NFT) e Fibra de Coco

  • Afonso, H.
  • Fanha, C.
  • Grosso, I.
  • Roque, C.

The hydroponic system (NFT and coconut fiber) is a growing technique without the presence of soil. This method was used to compare the growth of Eruca sativa in two different systems: NFT and coconut fiber. After analysing the experimental results, it was possible to conclude that Eruca sativa showed, on average, higher growth in the coconut fiber system. All plants were grown in a greenhouse using a predetermined solution with evident results.
The results led to the conclusion that the growth of Eruca sativa was greater in the coconut fiber, although the growth was very similar in both systems. At the beginning of the experiment, the plants did not show much development, but from the third and fourth weeks they showed significant growth.
It was possible to conclude that the hydroponics method is beneficial to the production and growth of Eruca sativa, since the plants grew more than the expected average, ultimately exceeding the group's expectations.

Keywords: Eruca sativa; Nutrient Film Technique; Coconut fiber; Growth; Systems; Experiments.






Comparação do Desenvolvimento Mentha pulegium em hidroponia por “Nutrient Film Technique”(NFT) e Fibra Coco

  • Cleto, G.
  • Coelho, D.
  • Pereira, B.
  • Ribeiro, G.

An experiment was conducted which consisted of raising and analyzing the growth of numerous specimens from the species Mentha pulegium. Over the course of several weeks, key data of the growth of the pennyroyal was extracted, including the height of the stem, the number of leaves and the presence of any kind of plagues or diseases while also gathering information on the external conditions that might affect the plants within the greenhouse, like its temperature, luminosity and humidity.
There were a total of twenty six specimens of M. pulegium, half were planted in the NFT hydroponic system (Nutrient Film Technique), while the other half was planted in coconut fiber. Plants in both systems were being monitored simultaneously and, in the end, their growth rates were compared.
Once the experiment was ultimately halted, it was concluded that the growth rate of the plants’ stem did not differ between methods, however, the number of leaves was higher in coconut fiber.

Keywords: Hydroponics; Mentha pulegium; Nutrient Film Techique; Coconut fiber.
